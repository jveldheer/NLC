Professional Techniques & Execution

Salmon Seasoning Guide

Seasoning That Actually Sticks

  1. Pre-salt: Pat the fillet bone dry. Salt both skin and flesh lightly. Let it sit for 10-20 minutes at room temperature until moisture beads appear.
  2. Dab, Do Not Wipe: Gently dab the moisture beads with a towel. Do not scrub the salt off. If you wiped hard, reapply a whisper of salt to the flesh side.
  3. Layer the Salt: Add a tiny pinch of fine salt to your basting glaze and finish with flake salt on the flesh side at the pass.

Honey Garlic Butter Punch (Quick Pan Glaze)

Reduce 2 tsp honey, 1 microplaned garlic clove, 1 tsp soy sauce, and 1 tsp white miso until syrupy. Pull off the heat, whisk in 1 tsp lemon juice, then mount 1 tbsp of cold butter cubes to make it glossy. Brush only on the flesh side after cooking.

Sear and Baste Timeline

  1. Pan on medium heat with a thin film of neutral oil.
  2. Place salmon skin-side down. Press gently with a fish spatula for 30-40 seconds to keep it flat.
  3. Cook skin-side down until doneness climbs about 4/5ths up the side. Tilt the pan and baste the flesh side with foaming butter for 30-45 seconds.
  4. Briefly "kiss" the flesh side on the pan for 15-30 seconds.
  5. Rest on a wire rack while making the glaze, then brush the glaze on the flesh side only.

Sauce Placement for Crispy Skin

  • Underpaint: Spoon a small pool of sauce on the plate and set the fish on top, skin-side up.
  • Around, not on: Dot or nappe the sauce around the fish.
  • Brush Finish: Brush the glaze on the flesh right before serving, keeping the skin dry.

Ramsay Grade Plating

  1. Warm plate. Swipe a small sauce base.
  2. Fish goes skin up, slightly off center.
  3. Brush glaze on flesh side.
  4. Add a small bright element for contrast on the side, like a lemon dressed herb salad.
  5. Finish with flake salt on the flesh side and one last micro swipe of lemon zest in the air.

Why it matters: Rushed plates look messy on camera and to judges.

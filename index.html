Salmon Seasoning Guide

Seasoning That Actually Sticks

  1. Pre salt: Pat the fillet bone dry. Salt both skin and flesh lightly and evenly. Let it sit 10-20 minutes at room temp. You will see beads of moisture rise.
  2. Dab, do not wipe: Gently dab the beads with a towel. Do not scrub off the salt. If you already wiped hard, reapply a whisper of salt to the flesh side only.
  3. Layer the salt: Add a tiny pinch of fine salt into your basting glaze and finish with a few flakes of salt on the flesh side at the pass. Three light touches beat one heavy one.

Make the Honey Garlic Butter Punch

Your first version was likely too sweet and too mild. Add umami, acid, and a pinch of heat.

Quick Pan Glaze:

  • 2 tsp honey
  • 1 small garlic clove, microplaned
  • 1 tsp soy or tamari
  • 1 tsp white miso
  • 1 tsp lemon juice or rice vinegar
  • 1 tbsp cold butter, cut in cubes
  • Optional pinch chili flake or black pepper

Reduce honey, garlic, soy, and miso over medium heat until syrupy and big slow bubbles form. Pull off heat, whisk in lemon, then mount the cold butter to make it glossy. Taste. It should read sweet, salty, garlicky, bright.

Brush this only on the flesh side after cooking. Do not put it on the skin.

Sear and Baste Timeline

  1. Pan medium heat with a thin film of neutral oil.
  2. Skin side down. Press gently with a fish spatula for 30-40 seconds so it stays flat.
  3. Cook skin side down until you see the doneness climb to about ‚Öò up the side. Tilt the pan and baste the flesh side with plain foaming butter for 30-45 seconds.
  4. Kiss the flesh side on the pan for 15-30 seconds or finish in a low oven if thick.
  5. Rest the fish on a wire rack while you make the glaze. Brush glaze on the flesh side only.

Where the Sauce Goes

  • Underpaint: Spoon or swoosh a small pool of sauce on the plate. Set the fish skin up on top so only flesh touches sauce.
  • Around, not on: Dot or nappe the sauce around the fish.
  • Brush finish: Brush the glaze on the flesh right before you walk. Skin stays dry.

Flavor Boosters That Play Great with Wild Alaskan Salmon

Pick one to add to the glaze or as a side relish. Small amounts go a long way.

  • A few drops of fish sauce to deepen savor
  • Lemon zest added off heat for lift
  • Grated fresh ginger for warmth
  • A spoon of chopped dill and chive folded in at the end
  • Tiny caper and toasted panko sprinkle on the plate, not on the skin

Ramsay Grade Plating

  1. Warm plate. Swipe a small sauce base.
  2. Fish goes skin up, slightly off center.
  3. Brush glaze on flesh side.
  4. Add a small bright element for contrast on the side, for example a lemon dressed herb salad or a few pickled shallots.
  5. Finish with flake salt on the flesh side and one last micro swipe of lemon zest in the air.

Quick Checklist for the Next Rep

  • Salt early. Dab moisture, do not wipe away seasoning.
  • Keep the skin completely dry from the moment it goes in the pan to the moment it hits the pass.
  • Put salt in the glaze. Brush the flesh only.
  • Sauce under or around. Never on the skin.

The Big Blind Spots and How to Close Them

1. Sauce Range Beyond Cream and Pan Reductions

Why it matters: In auditions many plates die from dryness or one note richness. Judges reward moisture and clarity with contrast.

Build This:

  • Beurre mont√© in 60 seconds: Splash of water to a simmer. Whisk in cold butter to a silky emulsion. Finish with lemon and cracked pepper.
  • Blender mayo or aioli in 90 seconds: Yolk plus acid plus salt, then stream oil. Flavor with garlic or herbs.
  • Salsa verde in 120 seconds: Fine chop herbs plus garlic plus acid plus oil plus salt.
  • Gastrique in 3-4 minutes: Reduce vinegar with a touch of sweet if available on the platform, mount with butter.

Drill: Three sauces in six minutes. Start a timer and produce beurre mont√©, salsa verde, and a quick peppercorn cream. Taste and write one line on what you would fix.

2. Fast Acid Work and Brightness on Command

Why it matters: Acid is the easiest way to create a professional finish under twenty minutes.

Build this: Always grab one acid and one herb. Make micro pickles when sugar appears. If no sugar, use salt and vinegar only.

Drill: Four minute acid sprint. Make lemon segments, a grated zest salt, a flash pickle of shaved veg, and a pan deglaze with vinegar. Plate each with a spoon of plain yogurt or cream to feel the balance.

3. Vegetable Command Under Ten Minutes

Why it matters: Veg is where many plates lose time and texture.

Build This:

  • Quick puree: Simmer veg in cream and salt five minutes. Blend hard. Mount butter.
  • Char and steam: Sear cut veg two minutes, splash water, cover two minutes, finish open one minute with oil and acid.

Drill: One veg two ways in eight minutes. Same vegetable as a puree and a char plus steam side. Plate both with your protein of the day.

4. Crisp Plus Creamy Plus Fresh Texture Triangle

Why it matters: Contrast sells speed cooking as intention, not compromise.

Build this: Every plate should hit a triangle. One crunch, one creamy or saucy element, one fresh bite.

Drill: Run any protein with toasted crumb or nut, a spoon sauce, and a raw herb salad or shaved veg.

5. Knife Speed and Accuracy for Even Cooking

Why it matters: Uneven cuts force you to choose between under and over.

Build This:

  • Chicken tender butterfly in ten seconds
  • Onion small dice 1 cup in ninety seconds
  • Pepper julienne in sixty seconds

Drill: Ten minute cut test. Film yourself. Score for speed and uniformity. Repeat twice weekly until times fall by twenty percent.

6. Fish and Odd Part Readiness

Why it matters: The platform loves collars, heads, and skin on fillets.

Build This:

  • Halibut or salmon collar cleanup and roast in twelve minutes
  • Cheeks off a head and pan roast in eight minutes
  • Pin bone removal with tweezers in sixty seconds

Drill: Buy one whole fish or a head and practice cheeks plus collar start to finish in fifteen minutes with a lemon butter finish.

7. Plan B and Plan C Thinking When the Grab Goes Sideways

Why it matters: You will not always get the protein you want.

Build this: Write a two line plan for each protein group that uses the same sauce and veg technique. Example: scallops sear or chicken cutlet shallow fry, both finish with lemon caper butter and charred peppers.

Drill: Blind bag platform. Put five slips in a bowl. Draw one protein and two veg. Start a twenty minute timer and cook without changing your station layout script.

8. The First Ninety Seconds of Station Setup

Why it matters: Early heat and layout decide your ceiling.

Build This:

  • Pan on and heating at second thirty
  • Sheet tray into any oven at minute one
  • Cutting board anchored and trash bowl placed
  • Salt, acid, oil, and tasting spoon within one reach

Drill: Dry run setup three times in a row. No food. Hit the same layout each time in under ninety seconds.

9. Plating Speed and Composition

Why it matters: Rushed plates read messy on camera and to judges.

Build This:

  • Sauce under protein or gloss on top, not both
  • Odd numbers of small items
  • One fresh green for life and scent

Drill: Ninety second plating test. Give yourself a white plate, a squeeze bottle, and a spoon. Plate three ways. Choose the cleanest and take a photo. Repeat daily for one week.

10. On Camera Narration While You Work

Why it matters: Mentors and judges want to hear a plan and progress. It also steadies your mind under pressure.

Build this: A fifteen second script you can say while moving.

Template: "I grabbed salmon, peppers, and lemon. I am searing to medium, making lemon butter, charring peppers, and finishing with herb and toasted crumb. I will plate sauce under for moisture and sheen."

Drill: Narrate the plan out loud at minutes zero, ten, and eighteen in every practice cook.

11. Dessert Contingency When a Dessert Brief Appears

Why it matters: You have focused on savory. A quick sweet can protect you if the challenge tilts that way.

Build This If Sugar Appears:

  • Crepe batter in two minutes and one pan cook
  • Caramelized fruit in butter with acid and herb
  • Whipped cream or sabayon in three to five minutes

Drill: Eight minute dessert. One pan crepes or butter roasted fruit with a flavored cream and a crumb.

12. Seasoning Math and Tasting Cadence

Why it matters: Under time stress most cooks under season and forget the final acid.

Build This:

  • Salt benchmarks. Soup or puree likes about 6g per liter (1¬º tsp per liter) start point, then adjust.
  • Taste three times. After sear, after reduction, at pass.

Drill: Make a base puree. Split into three bowls. Season one for salt only, one for salt and acid, one for salt, acid, and butter. Taste them back to back to lock in the finish you want.
